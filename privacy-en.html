Privacy Policy — Kabe

Effective: April 25, 2026

Kabe is a call-blocking app built around a simple promise — we do not collect, store, or transmit any of your data.

How Kabe works

Kabe registers a Call Directory Extension with iOS. When a call arrives, iOS checks the incoming number against Kabe's rule set entirely on your device, and Kabe returns a block-or-allow decision. The number itself never leaves your device.

What is stored on your device

  • Your language preference.
  • Your custom block rules (prefixes you've added).
  • Your exception list / whitelist entries.
  • Basic settings (feature toggles, last-opened screen, etc.).

These are saved in your device's standard app storage and the App Group container. They are synced to iCloud only if you have iOS's standard app-data sync enabled for your Apple ID. They are never sent to the developer or any third party.

What leaves your device

Nothing, with one exception: if you choose to make an In-App Purchase (Tip Jar), the transaction is handled entirely by Apple's StoreKit. Kabe only receives a success/failure result — we do not see your payment details, your Apple ID, or your purchase history. See Apple's Privacy Policy for details.

No analytics, no tracking, no ads

Kabe contains zero analytics SDKs, tracking libraries, advertising identifiers, or crash reporting services. The developer has no way to know whether you open the app, how often you use it, or what rules you configure.

Children's privacy

Kabe does not collect any personal information and is suitable for users of all ages.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a new effective date.
